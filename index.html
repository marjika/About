Why I code

I have always loved logical thought--math and science were my favorite subjects in school. In my younger days, I loved pure science and exploring ideas, but as I've grown older, I love more the thought of making something, using science. Engineering and problem solving allow me to use my mental talents as well as my creative desires. My husband, a software developer and architect, introduced me to coding. Coding is my outlet for my need to learn, build, explore ideas, and connect with people and concepts.

People I love

Before I was a coding genius, I spent many years raising four amazing, smart, and talented children. Whether it's traveling, playing games, making cookies, or going to a musical together, I love to make memories with all my family. Those years spent raising kids and volunteering in a number of ways taught me to value people, trust in the future, love unconditionally, and enjoy the little things.

Places I love

Warm places in winter and cool places in summer, any beautiful view in the world, my garden, surrounded by flowers, my couch with a crossword puzzle to do, my dining room table with a game or puzzle and my family, the piano playing a hymn or classical music, and at my computer learning and creating code. Other favorite places include but are not limited to: San Diego, Yellowstone, Arches National Park, the beach, the mountains, the lake, and especially our own local canal in the spring with the ducklings swimming with their families.
